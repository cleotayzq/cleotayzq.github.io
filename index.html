Research

"And yet what are we to do about this terribly significant business of other people ... so ill equipped are we all to envision one another's interior workings and invisible aims? You get them wrong before you meet them, while you're anticipating meeting them; you get them wrong while you're with them; and then you go home to tell somebody else about the meeting and you get them wrong all again... That's how we know we're alive: we're wrong."

My primary research interest is theory of mind.
In layperson's terms, theory of mind allows you to put yourself in someone else’s shoes and think about what they’re thinking or feeling. Using psych jargon, theory of mind refers to the ability to represent and attribute mental states such as beliefs, desires, and intentions to oneself and to others.

I'm also curious about the development of theory of mind, its relation to language, and how it helps us to navigate the social world (e.g., detecting deception and deciding whom to trust/learn from).
